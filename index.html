BLOWBACK AIR GUNS

WHAT IS BLOWBACK?

In the airgunning world, you will often hear the term blowback or non-blowback when someone is referring to an airgun pistol. So what exactly is blowback on an air powered gun? Blowback refers to the slide, or in some cases the bolt of the air gun that moves back and forth with each shot. The slide or bolt is moved rearwards in a blowback airgun by CO2 or green gas, depending on your airgun's power system. This blowback motion simulates the recoil of a firearm. Non-blowback replica airguns have a fixed slide and are sometimes one solid molded or cast piece.

WHY SHOOT WITH BLOWBACK?

The best benefit of a blowback action pistol is realism. With simulated recoil, near-identical shape, weight, feel, and similar mechanics and controls to a firearm, blowback airgun pistols are a budget-friendly option for someone who is wanting to get more trigger time without exhausting their ammo reserves. Think "practice" with airsoft and airguns. Plus, low-cost airgun ammunition is readily available.

The blowback action of Umarex® air pistols is better suited for action shooting than non-blowback options. The trigger designs are more true to its firearm counterpart and the full-size, drop-free magazines found in blowback airgun and airsoft pistols allow for excellent mag swap exercises.

Blowback airgun pistols let you practice more frequently and conveniently. In most cases, you can train in your own backyard*. Since many blowback air guns are replicas of firearms, you can use the same accessories like tactical lights, most standard holsters, etc. ‌Handgun trainers and trainees have discovered that the use of airsoft and air guns is an excellent tool to supplement training and practice exercises.
